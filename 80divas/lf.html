Luther Vandross

Lisa began her career with Luther Vandross. He was not only her first gig, but her longest. Lisa sang on every tour and album with Luther from the mid-80s until he stopped working, due a stroke which ended his recording career.

Luther was a world-class vocal producer and arranger, and known to only have quality talent behind him. He encouraged Lisa to pursue a solo career, as singers like Patti Labelle, David Bowie, and Roberta Flack had once pushed him.

“He saw me in a way that I couldn’t see myself,” she once shared. “He made me feel like a diamond though I felt like a grain of sand.”

Lisa found success with her 1991 debut album So Intense, which produced the Grammy Award–winning hit single "How Can I Ease the Pain". However, the pressures that came with being a woman in entertainment-to be thin and glamorous-were overwhelming. She was more comfortable where she started, in the background.


Lisa Fischer is one artist happy she didn’t become a bigger solo success. Instead, she became a sought-after session vocalist and background singer including joining the Rolling Stones on tour in 1989.

Lisa did finally get comfortable enough to launch a solo tour, but she performs now in draped, flowing garments, often in bare feet, always with bare face and natural hair. Easy, relaxed, in a way she can focus on the music and not the package.
